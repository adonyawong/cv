Published Work

In My Mind: The World through the Eyes of Autism (Tate Publishing, 2009) creatively explores the inner world of a child who is living with an Autism Spectrum Disorder diagnosis - the world no one else can see.

From exciting adventures to silly games and conversations friends, the main character invites readers to see how a child with autism sees the world, and, in turn, how the world often sees him.

My primary motivation for writing such a book stemmed from the most basic recognition that there was a scarcity of literature directed to and written for autistic children.

It is my intention, through my work, to encourage readers to want to learn more about autism. In My Mind was written to help raise awareness about autism, in addition to educating readers by offering them a different perception in what they may 'think' they are seeing when they are observing a child with autism. I hope In My Mind will strengthen readers' levels of compassion and tolerance for those “different” than themselves.
